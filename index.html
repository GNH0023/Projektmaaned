Exploring Your Weight Across the Solar System:

  • Mars: Mars, the red planet, has a weaker gravitational pull than Earth. As a result, you'll weigh less on Mars compared to your weight on Earth.
  • Jupiter: Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, boasts a massive gravitational force. Prepare to experience a significant increase in weight when you step onto this gas giant.
  • Venus: Venus, the hot and tumultuous neighbor of Earth, has a gravity similar to our planet. Expect your weight on Venus to be relatively close to your Earth weight.
  • Mercury: Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, has a weaker gravitational pull. Your weight on Mercury will be less than what you're accustomed to on Earth.
  • Saturn: Saturn, famous for its stunning rings, has a gravitational force that is slightly stronger than Earth's. You may find yourself feeling a bit heavier on this ringed giant.
  • Uranus and Neptune: As you venture towards the outer reaches of our solar system, Uranus and Neptune will subject you to weaker gravitational forces, resulting in a decrease in your weight compared to Earth
